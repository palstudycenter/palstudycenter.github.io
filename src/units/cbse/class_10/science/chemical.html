📘 Unit 1- Chemical reaction
Question - What is a Chemical Reaction?

Answer -

A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances undergo a chemical change to form new substances with different properties.

During a chemical reaction, atoms are rearranged but do not change into atoms of another element.

Question - What are the characteristics of a Chemical Reaction?

Answer -

The main characteristics of a chemical reaction are:

  • Evolution of gas.
  • Formation of a precipitate.
  • Change in colour.
  • Change in temperature.
  • Change in state.
Question - What is a Chemical Equation?

Answer -

The symbolic representation of a chemical reaction using chemical formulae is called a chemical equation.

Example:

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂

Question - What do the symbols (s), (l), (g) and (aq) represent in a chemical equation?

Answer -

  • (s) = Solid state
  • (l) = Liquid state
  • (g) = Gaseous state
  • (aq) = Aqueous solution
Question - State the Law of Conservation of Mass.

Answer -

The Law of Conservation of Mass states that mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction.

Therefore, the total mass of reactants is equal to the total mass of products.

Question - What is a Balanced Chemical Equation?

Answer -

A chemical equation in which the number of atoms of each element is equal on both sides of the equation is called a balanced chemical equation.

Example:

2Fe + 3H₂O → Fe₂O₃ + 3H₂

Question - Why should a chemical equation be balanced?

Answer -

A chemical equation should be balanced to satisfy the Law of Conservation of Mass.

The number of atoms of each element must be equal on both sides of the equation.

Question - What information does a balanced chemical equation provide?

Answer -

A balanced chemical equation provides information about:

  • Reactants and products.
  • Number of atoms and molecules involved.
  • Physical states of substances.
  • Conditions of the reaction.
Question - What is meant by a skeletal chemical equation?

Answer -

A chemical equation in which the number of atoms of elements is not equal on both sides is called a skeletal chemical equation.

Example:

H₂ + O₂ → H₂O

Question - What are Reactants and Products?

Answer -

The substances that take part in a chemical reaction are called reactants.

The new substances formed as a result of a chemical reaction are called products.

Example:

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Here, Magnesium and Oxygen are reactants, while Magnesium Oxide is the product.

Question - On the basis of heat, how many types of chemical reactions are there?

Answer -

On the basis of heat, chemical reactions are of two types:

  1. Exothermic Reactions
  2. Endothermic Reactions
Question - What is an Exothermic Reaction?

Answer -

An exothermic reaction is a chemical reaction in which heat energy is released along with the formation of products.

General Form:

Reactants → Products + Heat

Question - Give one example of an Exothermic Reaction.

Answer -

Burning of methane is an example of an exothermic reaction.

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat

Question - What is an Endothermic Reaction?

Answer -

An endothermic reaction is a chemical reaction that absorbs heat energy from its surroundings.

Reactants + Heat → Products

Question - Give one example of an Endothermic Reaction.

Answer -

Thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate is an example of an endothermic reaction.

CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂

Question - On the basis of nature, how many types of chemical reactions are there?

Answer -

On the basis of nature, chemical reactions are of four types:

  1. Combination Reaction
  2. Decomposition Reaction
  3. Displacement Reaction
  4. Double Displacement Reaction
Question - What is a Combination Reaction?

Answer -

A reaction in which two or more substances combine to form a single product is called a combination reaction.

Example:

2Mg(s) + O₂(g) → 2MgO(s)

Question - Give one example of a Combination Reaction.

Answer -

The burning of magnesium in oxygen to form magnesium oxide is a combination reaction.

2Mg(s) + O₂(g) → 2MgO(s)

Question - What is a Decomposition Reaction?

Answer -

A reaction in which a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances is called a decomposition reaction.

Example:

CaCO₃(s) → CaO(s) + CO₂(g)

Question - Give one example of a Decomposition Reaction.

Answer -

The thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate is a decomposition reaction.

CaCO₃(s) → CaO(s) + CO₂(g)

Question - What is thermal decomposition?

Answer -

A decomposition reaction carried out by heating is called thermal decomposition.

Example:

CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂

Question - Why are decomposition reactions called the opposite of combination reactions?

Answer -

In a combination reaction, two or more substances combine to form a single product.

In a decomposition reaction, a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances.

Therefore, decomposition reactions are the opposite of combination reactions.

Question - What is a Displacement Reaction?

Answer -

A reaction in which a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound is called a displacement reaction.

Example:

Zn(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → ZnSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)

Question - Give one example of a Displacement Reaction.

Answer -

When zinc metal is placed in copper sulphate solution, zinc displaces copper from the solution.

Zn(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → ZnSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)

Question - What is a Double Displacement Reaction?

Answer -

A reaction in which two compounds exchange their ions to form two new compounds is called a double displacement reaction.

Example:

AgNO₃(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO₃(aq)

Question - Give one example of a Double Displacement Reaction.

Answer -

When silver nitrate solution is mixed with sodium chloride solution, a white precipitate of silver chloride is formed.

AgNO₃(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO₃(aq)

Question - Why is a Double Displacement Reaction also called a Precipitation Reaction?

Answer -

A double displacement reaction is called a precipitation reaction because an insoluble solid called a precipitate is formed during the reaction.

Example: Silver chloride (AgCl) formed in the reaction is a white precipitate.

Question - What is a Precipitation Reaction?

Answer -

A reaction in which an insoluble solid called precipitate is formed is known as a precipitation reaction.

Example:

AgNO₃(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO₃(aq)

Question - What is a Redox Reaction?

Answer -

A chemical reaction in which oxidation and reduction take place simultaneously is called a redox reaction.

Example:

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O

Question - What is Oxidation?

Answer -

The addition of oxygen or the removal of hydrogen from a substance is called oxidation.

Example:

2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO

Question - Why does magnesium ribbon need to be cleaned before burning?

Answer -

Magnesium ribbon is cleaned before burning to remove the thin layer of magnesium oxide present on its surface.

This oxide layer prevents magnesium from burning easily.

Question - What do you observe when magnesium ribbon is burnt in air?

Answer -

Magnesium burns with a dazzling white flame and forms a white powder of magnesium oxide.

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Question - What is Reduction?

Answer -

The removal of oxygen or the addition of hydrogen to a substance is called reduction.

Example:

CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O

Question - What is an Oxidising Agent?

Answer -

A substance that provides oxygen or removes hydrogen from another substance is called an oxidising agent.

In the reaction CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O, CuO acts as the oxidising agent.

Question - What is a Reducing Agent?

Answer -

A substance that provides hydrogen or removes oxygen from another substance is called a reducing agent.

In the reaction CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O, H₂ acts as the reducing agent.

Question - What are the effects of oxidation reactions in everyday life?

Answer -

The two common effects of oxidation reactions in everyday life are:

  1. Corrosion
  2. Rancidity
Question - What is Corrosion?

Answer -

Corrosion is the process in which metals are gradually damaged by the action of air, moisture, or chemicals on their surface.

Rusting of iron is an example of corrosion.

Question - What is Rusting?

Answer -

The corrosion of iron due to the action of air and moisture is called rusting.

Example:

Iron + Oxygen + Water → Rust

Question - How can corrosion be prevented?

Answer -

Corrosion can be prevented by:

  • Painting the metal surface.
  • Applying oil or grease.
  • Galvanisation.
  • Electroplating.
Question - What is Galvanisation?

Answer -

Galvanisation is the process of coating iron or steel with a thin layer of zinc to prevent rusting.

It protects the metal from contact with air and moisture.

Question - What is Rancidity?

Answer -

The oxidation of fats and oils present in food, resulting in unpleasant smell and taste, is called rancidity.

Question - How can rancidity be prevented?

Answer -

Rancidity can be prevented by:

  • Storing food in airtight containers.
  • Keeping food away from light and heat.
  • Adding antioxidants.
  • Packing food in nitrogen gas.
Question - What are antioxidants?

Answer -

Antioxidants are substances that prevent or slow down the oxidation of food materials.

Examples: BHA (Butylated Hydroxy Anisole) and BHT (Butylated Hydroxy Toluene).

Question - Why are chips packets flushed with nitrogen gas?

Answer -

Chips packets are flushed with nitrogen gas to prevent the oxidation of fats and oils present in the chips.

This helps in preventing rancidity and increases the shelf life of the food.

Question - Why is the food containing fats and oils stored in airtight containers?

Answer -

Food containing fats and oils is stored in airtight containers to prevent contact with oxygen present in air.

This helps in preventing rancidity and keeps the food fresh for a longer time.
