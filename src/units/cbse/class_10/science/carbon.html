📘 Unit-4 Carbon and compounds
Question - What is carbon?

Answer -

Carbon is a non-metallic element with the symbol C and atomic number 6.

It is one of the most important elements because all living organisms are made up of carbon compounds.

Carbon forms a very large number of compounds due to its unique properties such as tetravalency and catenation.

Question - Why is carbon called the element of life?

Answer -

Carbon is called the element of life because all living organisms contain carbon compounds.

Biomolecules such as carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins and nucleic acids are carbon compounds.

Question - Write the electronic configuration of carbon.

Answer -

The atomic number of carbon is 6.

Therefore, its electronic configuration is:

2, 4

Carbon has four electrons in its outermost shell.

Question - Why does carbon form covalent bonds?

Answer -

Carbon has four valence electrons.

It cannot lose four electrons because it requires a very large amount of energy.

It also cannot gain four electrons because the nucleus cannot hold ten electrons effectively.

Therefore, carbon completes its octet by sharing electrons with other atoms and forms covalent bonds.

Question - What is a covalent bond?

Answer -

A covalent bond is a chemical bond formed by the mutual sharing of one or more pairs of electrons between two atoms.

Example:

H₂, Cl₂, O₂, CH₄ and CO₂ are covalent compounds.

Question - State the properties of covalent compounds.

Answer -

The properties of covalent compounds are:

  • They generally have low melting and boiling points.
  • They are poor conductors of electricity.
  • They are usually insoluble in water.
  • They are soluble in organic solvents.
  • They consist of molecules formed by sharing of electrons.
Question - Why does carbon not form ionic compounds?

Answer -

Carbon does not form ionic compounds because:

  • It cannot lose four electrons easily due to very high energy requirement.
  • It cannot gain four electrons because this is highly unstable.
  • Therefore, carbon shares electrons instead of transferring them.
Question - Why does carbon form a large number of compounds?

Answer -

Carbon forms a very large number of compounds due to the following two properties:

  • Tetravalency – Carbon forms four covalent bonds.
  • Catenation – Carbon atoms join with one another to form long chains and rings.
Question - What is tetravalency of carbon?

Answer -

The property by which carbon forms four covalent bonds is called tetravalency.

Since carbon has four electrons in its outermost shell, it requires four more electrons to complete its octet.

Hence, the valency of carbon is 4.

Question - What is catenation?

Answer -

The property of carbon by which carbon atoms join with one another through covalent bonds to form long chains, branched chains and rings is called catenation.

This property is responsible for the formation of a very large number of carbon compounds.

Question - Explain the formation of covalent bond using electron dot structure.

Answer -

A covalent bond is formed by the mutual sharing of one or more pairs of electrons between two atoms so that each atom attains a stable electronic configuration (duplet or octet).

The shared pair of electrons is represented by electron dots. This representation is called the electron dot structure.

Carbon forms covalent bonds because it cannot lose or gain four electrons easily. Instead, it shares electrons with other atoms to complete its octet.

Question - What is an electron dot structure?

Answer -

The representation of the valence electrons of an atom by dots around its symbol is called the electron dot structure or Lewis dot structure.

It helps to show the transfer or sharing of electrons during the formation of chemical bonds.

Question - Explain the formation of methane (CH₄) by electron dot structure.

Answer -

Carbon has four valence electrons, while each hydrogen atom has one valence electron.

Carbon shares one electron with each of the four hydrogen atoms. Thus, four single covalent bonds are formed and both carbon and hydrogen attain stable electronic configuration.

Molecular formula: CH₄

Methane is the simplest saturated hydrocarbon (alkane).

Question - Draw the electron dot structure of hydrogen molecule (H₂).

Answer -

Each hydrogen atom has one valence electron. Both hydrogen atoms share one electron each to complete their duplet.

Question - Draw the electron dot structure of chlorine molecule (Cl₂).

Answer -

Each chlorine atom has seven valence electrons. Both chlorine atoms share one pair of electrons to complete their octet.

Question - Draw the electron dot structure of oxygen molecule (O₂).

Answer -

Each oxygen atom has six valence electrons. Both oxygen atoms share two pairs of electrons and form a double covalent bond.

Question - What is the versatile nature of carbon?

Answer -

Carbon is called a versatile element because it forms a very large number of compounds.

This versatility is mainly due to the following two properties:

  • Catenation – Carbon atoms can bond with one another to form long chains, branched chains and rings.
  • Tetravalency – Carbon forms four stable covalent bonds with carbon and other elements such as hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen and chlorine.

These properties enable carbon to form millions of organic compounds.

Question - What is allotropy?

Answer -

The phenomenon in which an element exists in two or more different forms having different physical properties but similar chemical properties is called allotropy.

The different forms are called allotropes.

Question - Name the allotropes of carbon.

Answer -

The main allotropes of carbon are:

  • Diamond
  • Graphite
  • Fullerene
Question - What is diamond?

Answer -

Diamond is an allotrope of carbon in which each carbon atom is covalently bonded to four other carbon atoms, forming a rigid three-dimensional network.

It is the hardest naturally occurring substance.

Question - Write the properties of diamond.

Answer -

  • Diamond is the hardest natural substance.
  • It is transparent and lustrous.
  • It is a bad conductor of electricity.
  • It has a very high melting point.
  • Each carbon atom is bonded to four other carbon atoms by strong covalent bonds.
Question - Write the uses of diamond.

Answer -

  • It is used in jewellery.
  • It is used for cutting glass.
  • It is used in rock drilling and mining equipment.
  • It is used for making cutting and polishing tools.
Question - What is graphite?

Answer -

Graphite is an allotrope of carbon in which each carbon atom is covalently bonded to three other carbon atoms, forming hexagonal layers.

The layers are held together by weak forces and can slide over one another easily.

Question - Write the properties of graphite.

Answer -

  • Graphite is a soft and slippery solid.
  • It is blackish-grey in colour.
  • It is a good conductor of electricity.
  • It has a layered hexagonal structure.
  • The layers can slide over one another due to weak intermolecular forces.
  • It has a high melting point.
Question - Write the uses of graphite.

Answer -

  • It is used in making pencil leads.
  • It is used as a lubricant in machines.
  • It is used for making electrodes.
  • It is used in electric furnaces and dry cells.
Question - Why is graphite a good conductor of electricity but diamond is not?

Answer -

In graphite, each carbon atom forms three covalent bonds and one electron remains free.

These free electrons move through the layers and conduct electricity.

In diamond, each carbon atom forms four covalent bonds. Therefore, no free electrons are available for conduction.

Question - Why do diamond and graphite have different physical properties although both are made of carbon?

Answer -

Diamond and graphite have different physical properties because their carbon atoms are arranged differently.

Diamond has a rigid three-dimensional network in which every carbon atom is bonded to four other carbon atoms.

Graphite consists of layers in which each carbon atom is bonded to three other carbon atoms. These layers can slide over one another due to weak forces.

Therefore, diamond is very hard whereas graphite is soft and slippery.

Question - What is fullerene?

Answer -

Fullerene is an allotrope of carbon in which carbon atoms are arranged in a hollow spherical, ellipsoidal or tubular structure.

The most common fullerene is C₆₀, also called Buckminsterfullerene.

Question - Write the uses of fullerene.

Answer -

  • It is used in nanotechnology.
  • It is used in medicines and drug delivery systems.
  • It is used as a lubricant.
  • It is used in superconducting and electronic materials.
Question - What are hydrocarbons?

Answer -

Hydrocarbons are organic compounds made up of only two elements, carbon (C) and hydrogen (H).

They are the simplest organic compounds and serve as the basic compounds for many other organic substances.

Examples: Methane (CH₄), Ethane (C₂H₆), Ethene (C₂H₄) and Ethyne (C₂H₂).

Question - How are hydrocarbons classified?

Answer -

Hydrocarbons are mainly classified into two types:

  • Saturated hydrocarbons (Alkanes) – They contain only single covalent bonds (C–C).
  • Unsaturated hydrocarbons – They contain one or more double (C=C) or triple (C≡C) bonds.

Unsaturated hydrocarbons are further classified into:

  • Alkenes (contain double bonds)
  • Alkynes (contain triple bonds)
Question - What are saturated hydrocarbons?

Answer -

Hydrocarbons in which all carbon atoms are connected only by single covalent bonds are called saturated hydrocarbons.

They are also called alkanes.

General formula:

CnH2n+2

Examples:

  • CH₄ – Methane
  • C₂H₆ – Ethane
  • C₃H₈ – Propane
  • C₄H₁₀ – Butane
Question - What are unsaturated hydrocarbons?

Answer -

Hydrocarbons containing one or more double (C=C) or triple (C≡C) bonds between carbon atoms are called unsaturated hydrocarbons.

They are of two types:

  • Alkenes – Contain at least one double bond.
  • Alkynes – Contain at least one triple bond.
Question - What are alkanes?

Answer -

Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons in which all carbon atoms are connected by single covalent bonds.

General formula:

CnH2n+2

Examples:

  • CH₄ – Methane
  • C₂H₆ – Ethane
  • C₃H₈ – Propane
  • C₄H₁₀ – Butane
Question - What are alkenes?

Answer -

Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons containing at least one double bond (C=C) between carbon atoms.

General formula:

CnH2n

Examples:

  • C₂H₄ – Ethene
  • C₃H₆ – Propene
  • C₄H₈ – Butene
Question - What are alkynes?

Answer -

Alkynes are unsaturated hydrocarbons containing at least one triple bond (C≡C) between carbon atoms.

General formula:

CnH2n-2

Examples:

  • C₂H₂ – Ethyne
  • C₃H₄ – Propyne
  • C₄H₆ – Butyne

Question - What is a homologous series?

Answer -

A homologous series is a group of organic compounds having the same functional group and similar chemical properties, in which the successive members differ by a –CH₂– group.

All the members of a homologous series can be represented by the same general formula.

Example (Alkanes):

  • CH₄ – Methane
  • C₂H₆ – Ethane
  • C₃H₈ – Propane
  • C₄H₁₀ – Butane
Question - Write the characteristics of a homologous series.

Answer -

The important characteristics of a homologous series are:

  • All members have the same functional group.
  • All members have similar chemical properties.
  • Successive members differ by one –CH₂– group.
  • The molecular mass of consecutive members differs by 14 u.
  • Physical properties such as melting point and boiling point show a gradual change with increase in molecular mass.
  • All members can be represented by the same general formula.
Question - What is a functional group?

Answer -

An atom or a group of atoms present in an organic compound that determines its characteristic chemical properties is called a functional group.

Compounds having the same functional group show similar chemical properties.

Question - Write the chemical properties of carbon compounds.

Answer -

The important chemical properties of carbon compounds are:

  1. Combustion (Burning)
  2. Oxidation
  3. Addition Reaction
  4. Substitution Reaction
Question - What is combustion? Write the combustion reaction of methane.

Answer -

The process in which a carbon compound burns in oxygen to produce carbon dioxide, water and a large amount of heat and light is called combustion.

Chemical equation:

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat + Light

Combustion reactions are exothermic reactions because they release heat.

Question - Why are carbon compounds used as fuels?

Answer -

Carbon compounds are used as fuels because:

  • They burn easily in oxygen.
  • They produce a large amount of heat energy.
  • They have high calorific value.
  • Their combustion can be controlled easily.
Question - What is oxidation? Explain the oxidation of ethanol.

Answer -

The process in which oxygen is added or hydrogen is removed from a substance is called oxidation.

Ethanol is oxidised to ethanoic acid by oxidising agents such as alkaline potassium permanganate (KMnO₄) or acidified potassium dichromate (K₂Cr₂O₇).

Chemical equation:

CH₃CH₂OH + 2[O] → CH₃COOH + H₂O

Question - What are oxidising agents? Give two examples.

Answer -

Oxidising agents are substances that supply oxygen or remove hydrogen from another substance.

Examples:

  • Alkaline potassium permanganate (KMnO₄)
  • Acidified potassium dichromate (K₂Cr₂O₇)
Question - What is an addition reaction?

Answer -

The reaction in which atoms or groups of atoms are added across the double or triple bond of an unsaturated hydrocarbon to form a saturated compound is called an addition reaction.

Example (Hydrogenation):

CH₂=CH₂ + H₂ → CH₃–CH₃

(Nickel catalyst)

Question - What is hydrogenation? Write its industrial application.

Answer -

The addition of hydrogen to an unsaturated hydrocarbon in the presence of a catalyst such as nickel is called hydrogenation.

Chemical equation:

CH₂=CH₂ + H₂ → CH₃–CH₃

(Nickel catalyst)

Industrial application:

Hydrogenation is used to convert vegetable oils into vanaspati ghee.

Question - What is a substitution reaction?

Answer -

A reaction in which one atom or group of atoms in a compound is replaced by another atom or group of atoms is called a substitution reaction.

Saturated hydrocarbons generally undergo substitution reactions.

Example:

CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl

(In the presence of sunlight)

Question - What is ethanol?

Answer -

Ethanol (C₂H₅OH) is the second member of the homologous series of alcohols.

It is commonly known as ethyl alcohol.

Ethanol contains the hydroxyl (-OH) functional group.

  • It is completely miscible with water.
  • It has a boiling point of about 78°C.
  • It is a neutral substance and has no effect on red or blue litmus paper.
  • It is a good solvent for many organic substances.

The important chemical properties of ethanol are:

  1. Combustion
  2. Oxidation
  3. Reaction with sodium
  4. Esterification

Ethanol burns in air to produce carbon dioxide, water and a large amount of heat.

CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH ⇌ CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O

Answer -

Ethanol mixed with poisonous substances such as methanol, pyridine or copper sulphate to make it unfit for drinking is called denatured alcohol.

Denatured alcohol contains poisonous chemicals such as methanol and pyridine.

CH₃CH₂OH → CH₂=CH₂ + H₂O

Condition: Conc. H₂SO₄, 443 K

Question - What is dehydration?

Answer -

The removal of a water molecule (H₂O) from a compound is called dehydration.

For example, ethanol undergoes dehydration to form ethene in the presence of concentrated sulphuric acid.

Question - What is Ethanoic Acid?

Pure ethanoic acid is called glacial acetic acid.

The important chemical properties of ethanoic acid are:

Question - What are Soaps?

Answer -

Soaps are the sodium or potassium salts of long-chain fatty acids.

They are prepared by heating animal fats or vegetable oils with sodium hydroxide (NaOH) or potassium hydroxide (KOH).

Question - What is Saponification?

Answer -

The process of preparing soap by heating fats or oils with sodium hydroxide or potassium hydroxide is called saponification.

Fat/Oil + NaOH → Soap + Glycerol

Question - What are Detergents?

Answer -

Detergents are synthetic cleansing agents generally prepared from petroleum products.

They clean effectively in both soft water and hard water because they do not form insoluble scum.

Question - What are Micelles?

Answer -

Soap molecules arrange themselves into spherical structures called micelles in water.

In a micelle, the hydrophobic (water-repelling) tails remain towards the dirt or grease, while the hydrophilic (water-attracting) heads remain towards water.

Thus, grease gets trapped inside the micelle and is washed away with water.

Question - Explain the cleansing action of soap.

Answer -

A soap molecule has two ends:

  • Hydrophobic tail – attracts oil and grease.
  • Hydrophilic head – attracts water.

When soap is added to water, the hydrophobic tails surround the oily dirt and form micelles. The hydrophilic heads remain in water. As a result, dirt is lifted from the surface and washed away with water.

Question - Why do soaps not work well in hard water?

Answer -

Hard water contains calcium (Ca²⁺) and magnesium (Mg²⁺) ions.

These ions react with soap to form an insoluble precipitate called scum, which reduces the cleansing action of soap.

Question - Why are detergents preferred over soaps in hard water?

Answer -

Detergents do not react with calcium and magnesium ions present in hard water.

Therefore, they do not form scum and clean more effectively than soaps in hard water.

Answer -

Many synthetic detergents are not easily decomposed, therefore they remain in water bodies for a long time and cause pollution.
